Ability Scores
Every creature has six ability scores. Each has a modifier = (score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down. Your modifier is what you add to dice rolls.
STR — Strength
Raw physical power. Melee attacks, carrying & lifting, breaking things.
Skills: Athletics
DEX — Dexterity
Agility & reflexes. Ranged attacks, AC (light/no armor), initiative, finesse weapons.
Skills: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
CON — Constitution
Endurance & toughness. Hit point calculations, concentration checks.
No associated skills.
INT — Intelligence
Memory & reasoning. Wizard spellcasting. Identifying spells and creatures.
Skills: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion
WIS — Wisdom
Perception & willpower. Cleric, Druid, Ranger spellcasting. Noticing the world.
Skills: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival
CHA — Charisma
Force of personality. Bard, Paladin, Sorcerer, Warlock spellcasting. Social influence.
Skills: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion
Skills Reference
Acrobatics DEX
Tumbling, balancing, escaping grapples, and staying on your feet in tricky situations.
Animal Handling WIS
Calming, directing, controlling, or reading the intentions of animals.
Arcana INT
Recalling lore about spells, magic items, symbols, magical traditions, and planar beings.
Athletics STR
Climbing, jumping, and swimming in difficult conditions. Grappling and shoving.
Deception CHA
Convincingly lying, disguising intent, misleading others with false statements or actions.
History INT
Recalling lore about historical events, legendary people, ancient kingdoms, past disputes.
Insight WIS
Reading a creature's true intentions, detecting lies, sensing emotions behind words.
Intimidation CHA
Influencing others through threats, hostile actions, or a menacing physical presence.
Investigation INT
Searching for clues, deducing from evidence, finding hidden objects or weak points.
Medicine WIS
Diagnosing illnesses, stabilizing a dying creature, predicting how a disease will progress.
Nature INT
Recalling lore about terrain, plants, animals, weather, and natural cycles.
Perception WIS
Noticing things in your environment using sight, hearing, or other senses. The most used skill!
Performance CHA
Entertaining an audience through music, dance, acting, storytelling, or another art form.
Persuasion CHA
Influencing someone through tact, charm, flattery, or reasoned argument.
Religion INT
Recalling lore about deities, rites, prayers, religious hierarchies, and holy symbols.
Sleight of Hand DEX
Pickpocketing, palming objects, planting items on others, and manual trickery.
Stealth DEX
Moving silently, staying in shadows, hiding from creatures that haven't noticed you.
Survival WIS
Tracking creatures, foraging for food, navigating wilderness, predicting weather.
Movement & Exploration
Walking
Normal travel pace. Fast pace = better distance, −5 to Perception. Slow pace = worse distance, can Stealth.
Climbing
Costs 2 ft of movement per 1 ft climbed unless you have a climb speed. Difficult surfaces may require an Athletics check.
Swimming
Costs 2 ft per 1 ft unless you have a swim speed. Rough or stormy water may require an Athletics check.
Long Jump
Running (10+ ft): leap up to your STR score in feet. Standing: half that. Requires Athletics check to clear low obstacles.
High Jump
Running (10+ ft): reach up to 3 + your STR modifier in feet. Standing: half that.
Difficult Terrain
Every 1 ft traveled in difficult terrain costs 2 ft of movement. Examples: thick mud, rubble, deep snow, shallow water.
Dropping Prone
Free action. While prone: crawling costs 2 ft per 1 ft. Standing up costs half your movement.
Resting
Short Rest 1 Hour
Spend one or more Hit Dice to recover HP. Some class features (e.g. Warlock spell slots, Fighter's Action Surge) also refresh on a short rest.
Long Rest 8 Hours
Recover all HP and half your total Hit Dice. Most class features and all spell slots refresh. Can only benefit once per 24 hours.
Combat
Action Economy
Action 1 per turn
Your main thing each turn. Attack, cast a spell, dash, dodge, help, hide, ready, search, use an object, grapple, shove, or anything else the DM allows.
Bonus Action 1 per turn
Only usable if a feature, spell, or ability gives you one. You can't spend a bonus action unless something specifically lets you. Examples: off-hand attack, Cunning Action (Rogue), healing word.
Reaction 1 per round
Used in response to a specific trigger, even on someone else's turn. Resets at the start of your turn. Examples: opportunity attack, Counterspell, Shield spell, Uncanny Dodge.
Movement Up to Speed
Move up to your speed in feet per turn. You can split movement before, after, or between your actions. Moving through difficult terrain costs 2 ft per 1 ft.
Free Action / Object Interaction
Brief, simple tasks — speaking a few words, dropping something, drawing a weapon. You get one free object interaction per turn. Extra ones cost your Action.
Common Actions in Combat
Attack
Make one weapon attack (or unarmed strike). Some features give extra attacks. Roll to hit: d20 + ability mod + proficiency (if proficient) vs. target AC.
Cast a Spell
Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 Action. Spells with a bonus action casting time use your Bonus Action instead.
Dash
Gain extra movement equal to your speed this turn (effectively doubling your movement).
Disengage
Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of your turn.
Dodge
Until your next turn: attackers have disadvantage on attacks against you, and you have advantage on DEX saving throws. Only works if you can see the attacker and aren't incapacitated.
Help
Give an ally advantage on their next ability check, OR give an ally advantage on their next attack roll against a creature within 5 ft of you.
Hide
Make a Stealth (DEX) check. If successful and conditions allow (heavily obscured or behind cover), you become hidden.
Ready
Choose a trigger ("when the enemy steps through the door…") and an action to take as a Reaction when that trigger occurs.
Search
Make a Perception or Investigation check to locate something not immediately obvious.
Grapple
Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics (their choice). On success: target is grappled (speed = 0). You can drag them at half speed. Ends if you're incapacitated or move out of reach.
Shove
Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics. On success: push them 5 ft away OR knock them prone (your choice).
Opportunity Attack
Reaction. When a creature you can see leaves your melee reach without Disengaging, you can make one melee attack against them.
Conditions
Blinded
Auto-fails any check requiring sight. Attack rolls against you have advantage; your attack rolls have disadvantage.
Charmed
Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful effects. The charmer has advantage on Charisma checks against you.
Deafened
Can't hear. Auto-fails ability checks that require hearing.
Exhaustion Stacking
Level 1: Disadvantage on ability checks. Level 2: Speed halved. Level 3: Disadvantage on attacks & saves. Level 4: Max HP halved. Level 5: Speed = 0. Level 6: Death.
Frightened
Disadvantage on ability checks and attacks while the source of fear is in line of sight. Can't willingly move closer to it.
Grappled
Speed becomes 0 and can't be increased. Ends if grappler is incapacitated, or if the target is moved outside the grappler's reach.
Incapacitated
Can't take Actions or Reactions.
Invisible
Can't be seen without magic or special senses. Attacks against you have disadvantage; your attacks have advantage.
Paralyzed
Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Auto-fails STR and DEX saves. Attacks have advantage; hits within 5 ft are critical hits.
Petrified
Turned to stone. Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Weight increases tenfold. Resistant to all damage; immune to poison and disease. Auto-fails STR and DEX saves; attacks have advantage.
Poisoned
Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
Prone
Can only crawl (costs 2 ft per 1 ft). Your attacks have disadvantage. Melee attacks against you have advantage; ranged attacks have disadvantage. Standing up costs half your movement.
Restrained
Speed becomes 0. Attack rolls against you have advantage; your attack rolls have disadvantage. Disadvantage on DEX saving throws.
Stunned
Incapacitated, can't move, can speak only falteringly. Auto-fails STR and DEX saves. Attacks have advantage.
Unconscious
Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Drops held items and falls prone. Auto-fails STR and DEX saves. Attacks have advantage; hits within 5 ft are critical hits.
